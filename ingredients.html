Why these ingredients

A quick, evidence‑based rationale for the DIY gel recipe used in the calculator.

Carbohydrates 2:1 ratio

Maltodextrin (2 parts): Fast‑absorbing glucose source that provides steady energy without being overly sweet. It forms the bulk of the fuel to keep the gel easy on the stomach.

Fructose (1 part): Uses a different intestinal transporter than glucose, allowing higher total carb uptake per hour with less GI distress. The 2:1 maltodextrin:fructose ratio is a widely used endurance sweet spot for efficient absorption.

Takeaway: The 2:1 split helps you absorb more carbs per hour with fewer stomach issues compared to single‑sugar gels.

Citric acid for palatability

Flavor balance: A very small amount adds gentle tartness that cuts sweetness and reduces palate fatigue over long efforts.

Acidity tuning: Helps keep the mixture’s acidity in a comfortable range without being harsh.

Sodium targeting

Why sodium matters: Replaces what you lose in sweat to support fluid balance, nerve signaling, and muscle function during prolonged efforts.

How the level is set: The calculator targets about 300 mg sodium per 30 g carbs. This ties sodium to your fueling load so longer or higher‑intensity efforts—where you consume more carbs—scale sodium accordingly.

What the math does: Total carbs → sodium target → table salt grams via a conversion of roughly 390 mg sodium per gram of table salt (NaCl). The output shows both total sodium and the table salt needed to reach it.

Adjusting for you: If you’re a very salty sweater or very light sweater, you may prefer to tweak the sodium target. Future versions will let you customize this within the app.
